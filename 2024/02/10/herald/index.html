Plastic surgery is a branch of surgery that focuses on improving or repairing the shape and function of body parts, including parts of the face and body. This may include various procedures to improve the physical appearance, reduce the signs of aging, or restore the function of a part of the body after an injury or illness.

Plastic surgery can have positive effects on an individual's appearance and self-confidence, but it can also be associated with potential risks and consequences. Some of the effects of plastic surgery are as follows: Visual Enhancement; Increase in Self-Confidence; Repair of Injuries and Deformities; Function Improvement.

However, it is important to understand that there are also some dangerous aspects and possible consequences of plastic surgery: Surgical Risks; Aesthetic Consequences; Recovery Time; Emotional Experience The first succesful plastic surgery thought to have happened: Walter Yeo, a sailor injured at the Battle of Jutland, is assumed to have received plastic surgery in 1917.
